Transparent scoring

Methodology

The Small Business Support Priority Signal is a cautious public-data signal from 0 to 100. It is designed to support local verification, not to make decisions for local partners.

What the Support Priority Signal is

It is a county-level public-data conversation starter. Higher values indicate where available public data suggests local verification, resource mapping, or capital-navigation conversations may be useful.

What it is not

It is not a credit model, underwriting tool, lending recommendation, or measure of community value. It should be interpreted alongside local knowledge and source limitations.

Inputs and weights

  • 20% Small-business importance signal, including density, scale, and small-establishment share
  • 20% Capital-access visibility gap
  • 20% Community economic stress
  • 15% Housing/cost pressure
  • 15% Local support ecosystem gap
  • 10% Data-quality gap / verification need

Normalization

Indicators are converted to winsorized percentile scores from 0 to 100. Extreme values are clipped at the 5th and 95th percentiles before ranking. Directionality is explicit: lower branch density increases a visibility-gap signal, while higher rent burden increases a housing-pressure signal.

Missing data

If a component is missing, the final score uses available components and displays coverage. Missing official data is not filled with invented values. Optional and manual sources remain visible when unavailable.

Data quality grades

Grades summarize field completeness for the current build. Lower grades mean more local verification is needed before comparing signals or making program decisions.

Methodology flow

From scattered public sources to cautious briefs

1Source

Fetch official files or register manual imports.

2Validate

Standardize FIPS, fields, and source availability.

3Normalize

Apply winsorized percentiles with directionality.

4Publish

Generate static briefs with source notes and limitations.
