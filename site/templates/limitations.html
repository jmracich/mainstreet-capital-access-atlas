Limitations

This project is intentionally careful. Public data can help focus questions, but it cannot replace local knowledge or determine decisions.

This is not a credit model.

The signal is not designed for borrower, business, or loan-level decisions.

This is not an underwriting tool.

It should not be used to approve, deny, price, or prioritize any individual financial product.

This does not measure borrower creditworthiness.

County-level public data cannot evaluate individual businesses, owners, plans, or repayment capacity.

This does not direct lending or investment.

It does not determine where banks, CDFIs, governments, or nonprofits should lend, invest, or act.

Public data is lagged and incomplete.

Sources can be suppressed, revised, delayed, and uneven across geographies.

Data availability varies.

Optional, manual, and source-limited datasets may be unavailable in the current build.

County averages hide local variation.

Neighborhood-level conditions, business corridors, and rural access patterns can differ sharply within one county.

Local knowledge matters.

Residents, business owners, and community organizations understand context that dashboards cannot capture.

Use this as a conversation starter, not a final answer.

These public data signals may help identify places where local verification, partnership, coaching, capital-navigation support, or additional community support could be useful. They should not be the final word.
