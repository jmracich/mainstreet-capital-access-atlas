# Product Thesis

## Problem

Small-business support organizations have limited time and money. Public data on business density, capital access, lending visibility, household stress, and support infrastructure is scattered across federal datasets. As a result, local partners struggle to identify places where small-business need may be outpacing visible support capacity.

## User

Primary user: a local or regional small-business support partner deciding where to focus outreach, research, or partnership conversations.

Secondary users: CDFIs, chambers, local governments, community banks, SBDCs, universities, nonprofits, journalists, and civic-data volunteers.

## Decision this tool supports

Where should we investigate a possible small-business support gap?

## Output

The product generates:
1. a county support-gap diagnosis,
2. a peer comparison,
3. a local verification memo,
4. a transparent data-quality note,
5. downloadable open data.

## Non-goals

This is not a credit model, underwriting tool, grant allocator, bank rating system, or replacement for local knowledge.

## Success

A user should be able to identify one county worth follow-up, explain why it was flagged, and bring a verification memo to a local partner conversation within 10 minutes.
